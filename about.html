ABOUT CHELSEA

I'm a multimodal UX designer who enjoys getting in the field to conduct contextual research and engaging users in participatory design methods.

I'm in my last semester as a master's student at the University of Michigan School of Information, where I study human-computer interaction. I've particularly enjoyed designing computational hardware systems that are driven by gesture and voice interactions, but I'm also excited by discovering how new mediums and interaction modes can be utilized to grapple with the wicked questions of the world.

Previously, I studied psychology, film studies, and music at Bryn Mawr College, then taught English in Bangkok for three years. I discovered user experience design while working as a photo assistant in New York City, where I realized that UX sat at the intersection of my interests in human cognition and behavior, digital media, and education.
